Jog Falls, Karnataka

Jog Falls, located in the Shimoga district of Karnataka, is the second-highest plunge waterfall in India, with a height of 830 feet. Formed by the Sharavathi River, it cascades down in four distinct segments known as Raja, Rani, Roarer, and Rocket. The thunderous roar of water during the monsoon season creates a mesmerizing spectacle that attracts nature lovers, photographers, and adventure seekers from across the country.

The area surrounding the falls offers lush greenery, panoramic viewpoints, and trekking opportunities for visitors. The viewpoint near the falls provides a breathtaking aerial view, while a trek down to the base gives an up-close experience of the waterfall’s sheer power. It's an ideal destination for family picnics, photography, and nature walks.

Highlights:

  • Second-highest plunge waterfall in India
  • Four distinct streams: Raja, Rani, Roarer, and Rocket
  • Best monsoon views with heavy water flow
  • Panoramic viewpoints and trekking trails

Quick Info:

  • Location: Shimoga District, Karnataka
  • Height: 830 feet
  • Best Time to Visit: July–September (Monsoon Season)
  • Activities: Trekking, photography, bird watching

Travel Tips:

  • Visit during the monsoon for the best experience
  • Carry rain gear and non-slip footwear
  • Stay hydrated and bring snacks for the trek
  • Follow safety instructions while near the water
